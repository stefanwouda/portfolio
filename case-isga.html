ISGA 2016/2017

Helping international students to make the most out of their university life in Amsterdam

Reading time: 2 minutes

The municipality of Amsterdam believed that there was a lack of overview of sports activities happening in Amsterdam. I designed a mobile website that provides an overview of all the sports events happening in Amsterdam. During this short project, I was responsible for the design of the concept, the information architecture, and the UI of the website.

My role

The project was already defined when I started working on it. However, I did conduct brief research to clarify the problem a bit more. During the project, I was responsible for designing the information architecture and the corresponding wireframes for the concept. A bit of visual design was also part of my job.

About the ISGA

The ISGA is a book for exchange students to guide them through the city of Amsterdam. It consists of basic need-to-know facts at level 0 and then leads you from tourist highlights at level 1 to more special places at level 2 to hidden gems at level 3. The levels are indicated with the three Xs of Amsterdam’s coat of arms.

By students for students

The ISGA guide has been written, designed and produced for students by students of the international minor programme Publishing of the Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences.

Styleguide

Now it was clear where the new website could offer a solution for the inhabitants of Amsterdam, I grabbed pens and papers and started sketching different screens that could be part of the new website.

Presenting the final result

The product is a website for small-screen devices where exchange students can find places, like cafes and libraries, to work or study outside the campus.
